About Us

This website was created with the goal of increasing public awareness about crime in the City of Chicago through reliable and easy-to-understand data visualizations. By presenting complex information in a clear and accessible way, we aim to help individuals make informed decisions, stay safe, and engage in meaningful discussions about public safety and community well-being.

Thank you for visiting the site. We hope the insights provided here contribute to a better understanding of crime patterns and encourage positive change. If knowledge is power, then sharing it is the first step toward safer communities.

Statistics

What is the most frequently reported type of offense in Chicago?

Overall, the most frequently reported offenses in Chicago are consistently related to property and theft-based crimes, which appear far more often than other categories. While the total number of incidents fluctuates from year to year, these offense types remain dominant throughout the dataset. To better understand how crime levels change over time, the next visualization, “Crime by Type Over Time,” examines how the number of crimes evolves by date, highlighting long-term trends and periods of increase or decline.

How are arrest and non-arrest incidents for reported crimes distributed across years in Chicago?

This visualization compares crime incidents that resulted in arrests with those that did not across multiple years in Chicago. While both categories change over time, non-arrest incidents consistently outnumber arrests, highlighting a persistent gap between reported crimes and enforcement outcomes. Viewing these two outcomes side by side provides context for how arrest rates relate to overall crime levels and helps motivate further analysis of how different offense types contribute to these patterns in the next visualization.

How have crime levels changed over the past several years?

Over past years, crime levels in Chicago have shown a noticeable long-term decline. The data shown above indicates that crime peaked around 2008 and 2009, after which overall incident counts began to decrease steadily. This downward trend continues across the years, reaching its lowest level in 2025. These patterns suggest a sustained reduction in reported crime over time, providing important context for understanding changes in enforcement outcomes and offense types explored in the visualizations above.

Which districts experience the highest overall crime rates?

The visualization indicates that crime levels vary significantly across Chicago's council districts. Districts 3, 4, and 9 experience the highest overall crime rates, consistently recording more incidents than other areas, which suggests these districts face greater public safety challenges. In contrast, districts 6, 8, and 10 report the lowest crime counts, making them comparatively safer than the rest of the city. This contrast highlights how crime is concentrated in specific districts rather than evenly distributed, emphasizing the importance of location-based analysis when assessing crime and safety trends.

How are crimes distributed across different location types in Chicago?

This visualization illustrates how crime incidents are distributed across various location types in Chicago. The data shows that roads, highways, and streets account for the largest share of reported crimes, indicating that public and highly trafficked areas experience more criminal activity. Residential areas also record a substantial number of incidents, reflecting the impact of crime within neighborhoods. In contrast, locations such as colleges and other institutional areas report significantly fewer crimes. This distribution suggests that crime is more prevalent in open, accessible environments, while more controlled or monitored locations tend to experience lower incident rates.
